Research hub

AI Is Becoming Economic Infrastructure

The model layer gets attention. The infrastructure layer captures power.

The Institute researches the ownership, allocation, and institutional systems shaping the AI economy.

AI is not just software. It is infrastructure economics at planetary scale, shaped by compute, energy, chips, cloud concentration, capital allocation, governance capacity, institutional adaptation, and distribution control.

Core research areas

Area 01

Infrastructure & Compute Power

Research on compute concentration, hyperscaler dominance, chip supply chains, energy constraints, sovereign AI capacity, inference economics, cloud dependency, AI capital expenditure cycles, and ownership of intelligence infrastructure.

Intelligence scales where power, chips, and capital converge.
Area 02

Institutional Transformation

Research on how AI restructures governments, enterprises, universities, labor systems, decision-making, operating models, compliance structures, and governance capacity.

Institutions unable to absorb AI operationally will become administratively fragile.
Area 03

Distribution & Control Layers

Research on interfaces, AI workflows, enterprise lock-in, agent ecosystems, search disruption, trust layers, customer ownership, and AI-native distribution systems.

In AI markets, distribution compounds faster than capability.
