Framework

Enterprise AI Economics

From AI experiments to AI investment discipline.

Most organisations can track AI activity. Far fewer can measure AI value. Enterprise AI Economics gives leaders a structured way to inventory AI initiatives, evaluate value, govern portfolios, and make better investment decisions.

A practical framework for enterprise AI portfolios.

Enterprises are moving from isolated AI experiments to AI investment portfolios. As AI workflows, agents, copilots, and automation systems consume real operating cost, leaders need a structured way to decide which initiatives to scale, optimise, pause, or retire.

This is not a software product page. It is a public framework from the Institute for AI Economics for evaluating, governing, and allocating capital across enterprise AI initiatives.

Framework journey

From inventory to dynamic capital allocation.

Stage 1

AI Initiative Inventory & Value Scoring

Build a complete inventory of AI initiatives across the organisation and score each one against business value, feasibility, adoption, risk, and cost.

  • Identify all active and proposed AI initiatives
  • Map each initiative to a business owner
  • Define the business problem being solved
  • Estimate cost, effort, and operating model impact
  • Score expected value and delivery feasibility
  • Identify data, risk, compliance, and adoption dependencies
Stage 2

Portfolio Governance & Executive Reporting

Move from scattered AI projects to a governed portfolio with decision rights, executive reporting, and clear accountability.

  • Define executive ownership and decision rights
  • Create AI portfolio review cadence
  • Categorise initiatives as scale, optimise, incubate, pause, or retire
  • Track adoption, cost, risk, and measurable business outcomes
  • Establish reporting for CEO, CFO, CIO, and business unit leaders
Stage 3

Investment Recommendations

Use evidence from the AI portfolio to recommend where capital should be increased, reduced, redirected, or stopped.

  • Compare initiatives based on value, cost, risk, and adoption
  • Identify initiatives with strong ROI signals
  • Identify underperforming or duplicated initiatives
  • Recommend funding increases, optimisation, or retirement
  • Link AI investment decisions to business priorities
Stage 4

Dynamic Capital Allocation

The long-term vision is an enterprise capability where capital can be continuously redirected toward AI workflows and agents that create measurable value.

  • Connect AI performance data to investment decisions
  • Allocate additional capacity to high-performing workflows
  • Reduce funding for low-value or low-adoption initiatives
  • Support faster decision-making across sales, marketing, product, operations, and support
  • Build an AI capital allocation muscle inside the enterprise

Why this matters

AI is becoming an investment portfolio.

As enterprises rent intelligence through models, agents, cloud platforms, and GPU-backed services, AI becomes an operating cost and a capital allocation decision. The winners will not simply be the organisations with the most AI projects. They will be the organisations with the best system for deciding which AI capabilities deserve more investment.
