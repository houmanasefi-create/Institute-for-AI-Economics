Bridging Legal Interpretation and Formal Logic: Faithfulness, Assumption, and the Future of AI Legal Reasoning

Source: arXiv Artificial Intelligence

Area: AI research

Published: May 16, 2026

Strategic relevance score: 2/10

Read original source

Summary

arXiv:2605.14049v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: The growing adoption of large language models in legal practice brings both significant promise and serious risk. Legal professionals stand to benefit from AI that can reason over contracts, draft documents, and analyze sources at scale, yet the high-stakes nature of legal work demands a level of rigor that current AI systems do not provide. The central problem is not simply that LLMs hallucinate facts and references; it is that they systematically draw inferences that go beyond what the source text actually supports, presenting assumption-laden conclusions as if they were logically grounded. This proposal presents a neuro-symbolic approach to legal AI that combines the expressive power of large language models with the rigor of formal verification, aiming to make AI-assisted legal reasoning both capable and trustworthy, thus reducing the burden of manual verification without sacrificing the accountability that legal practice demands.

Core thesis

The research proposes a neuro-symbolic approach to enhance AI legal reasoning by integrating the strengths of large language models with the rigor of formal verification, addressing the critical issue of assumption-laden conclusions that undermine the trustworthiness of AI in legal applications.

Economic interpretation

This advancement in AI legal reasoning could reshape the legal services market by increasing productivity and reducing costs for legal professionals. However, it also raises concerns about the displacement of traditional legal roles, the redistribution of power within legal institutions, and the potential monopolization of legal AI technologies by large firms, impacting market competition and access to justice.

Five core mental models

  1. The interplay between language models and formal logic creates a new paradigm for reasoning that can enhance accountability in legal practices.
  2. The distinction between inference and assumption is crucial for understanding how AI can misinterpret legal texts, affecting the validity of AI-generated legal advice.
  3. Neuro-symbolic systems can serve as a bridge between qualitative legal analysis and quantitative verification, transforming how legal knowledge is codified and accessed.
  4. The integration of AI into legal reasoning processes may shift the burden of proof and responsibility from human lawyers to AI systems, challenging existing legal frameworks.
  5. AI’s ability to process vast amounts of legal data could lead to emergent patterns in legal reasoning that are not easily understandable or controllable by human practitioners.

Five places experts disagree

  1. The extent to which AI can genuinely replicate human legal reasoning versus merely simulating it through pattern recognition.
  2. The ethical implications of relying on AI for legal decisions, especially concerning accountability and liability in case of errors.
  3. The impact of AI on the accessibility of legal services, with some arguing it will democratize access while others fear it will exacerbate existing inequalities.
  4. The effectiveness of neuro-symbolic approaches compared to purely statistical methods in achieving reliable legal AI outcomes.
  5. The potential regulatory challenges that arise from integrating AI into legal frameworks and how these might be addressed by policymakers.
