Research signal 5

On Distinguishing Capability Elicitation from Capability Creation in Post-Training: A Free-Energy Perspective

Source: arXiv Artificial Intelligence

Area: AI research

Published: May 13, 2026

Read original source

Summary

arXiv:2605.08368v1 Announce Type: new Abstract: Debates about large language model post-training often treat supervised fine-tuning (SFT) as imitation and reinforcement learning (RL) as discovery. But this distinction is too coarse. What matters is whether a training procedure increases the probability of behaviors the pretrained model could already produce, or whether it changes what the model can practically reach. We argue that post-training research should distinguish between capability elicitation and capability creation. We make this distinction operational by introducing the notion of accessible support: the set of behaviors that a model can practically produce under finite budgets.
